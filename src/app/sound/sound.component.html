Introduction to “Using Sound”

The purpose of Using Sound is to help you feel better without changing the sound of the tinnitus itself. Below you can read about three types of sound that can be used to help with tinnitus.

Soothing Sound

What is Soothing Sound?
  • Any sound that has a soothing effect on you
  • Any sound that makes you feel better as soon as you hear it
  • Can be environmental sound, music, or speech
How can Soothing Sound help?
  • By giving you a sense of relief from stress and tension caused by tinnitus
    • (Note: Soothing Sound can give you that sense of relief without changing the sound of your tinnitus!)
When can I use Soothing Sound?
  • Any time tinnitus bothers you

Interesting Sound

What is Interesting Sound?
  • Sound that keeps your attention
  • Sound that you actively listen to
  • Can be environmental sound, music, or speech
How can Interesting Sound help?
  • By helping you shift your attention away from your tinnitus
When can Interesting Sound help?
  • When you do not need to concentrate on something else
  • When you want to relax or sleep

Interesting Sound and Sleep
Interesting sound can be a powerful way to get your mind off of your tinnitus. This helps some people relax enough to get to sleep. It might not be helpful for others. Be open to using sound in surprising or unusual ways to manage your tinnitus!

Background Sound

What is Background Sound?
  • Any sound that is neutral (not soothing, not interesting, not annoying)
  • Can be environmental sound, music, or speech
How Can Background Sound Help?
  • Our brains are “wired” to notice contrast.
There is a lot of contrast between tinnitus and a quiet room. Adding background sound to a quiet room reduces the contrast. The reduced contrast makes it easier to ignore tinnitus.

How does Contrast Reduction Make it Easier to Ignore Tinnitus?
Imagine a lit candle in a dark room. The candle is the only light in the room. There is sharp contrast between the bright candle and the dark room. Therefore the candle naturally attracts a lot of attention. Next, imagine the same lit candle, but now with the lights on in the room. The contrast between the candle and the room has been reduced. The candle is just as bright as before, but attracts less attention because now there is other light in the room along with the candle.


The same idea applies to tinnitus. Imagine tinnitus in a quiet room. Tinnitus in a room with no other sounds naturally attracts attention. If you add background sound, your tinnitus will be just as loud as before. However it won’t attract as much attention because of the other sound in the room along with the tinnitus. Adding background sound creates an environment where it is easier for the tinnitus to go unnoticed.


Note: Sometimes Background Sound helps right away. Sometimes it takes weeks or months before you notice it is helping.

When can I use Background Sound?
  • Any time your tinnitus bothers you
A constant background of sound can help you notice your tinnitus less often. Background Sound should always be set to a comfortable loudness level. The sound should become a natural part of your day.
